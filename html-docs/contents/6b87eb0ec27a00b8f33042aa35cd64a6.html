floorDiv

Description

Returns the largest (closest to positive infinity) {@code long} value that is less than or equal to the algebraic quotient. There is one special case, if the dividend is the {@linkplain Long#MINVALUE Long.MINVALUE} and the divisor is {@code -1}, then integer overflow occurs and the result is equal to {@code Long.MIN_VALUE}. <p> Normal integer division operates under the round to zero rounding mode (truncation). This operation instead acts under the round toward negative infinity (floor) rounding mode. The floor rounding mode gives different results from truncation when the exact result is negative. <p> For examples, see {@link #floorDiv(int, int)}. @param x the dividend @param y the divisor @return the largest (closest to positive infinity) {@code long} value that is less than or equal to the algebraic quotient. @throws ArithmeticException if the divisor {@code y} is zero @see #floorMod(long, long) @see #floor(double) @since 1.8
